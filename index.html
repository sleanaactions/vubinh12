Bảo tháp Lục Độ Đài Sen

Six Perfections Lotus Stupa
The architectural highlight of complex is Six Perfections Lotus Stupa, which is symmetrically arranged with Bodhi tree. Built at end of 20th century, it remains a highly symbolic structure, strictly adhering to the iconographic principles of Buddhism.
At the very top is the nine-tiered lotus pedestal (Cửu phẩm liên hoa), crafted from precious stone, symbolizing the nine supreme levels of the Pure Land and representing spiritual elevation along the inner journey.

The entire stupa carries a reddish-brown hue, harmonizing with the ancient brick tones and the overall architecture of the temple.
Tower parameters
The stupa is fifteen meters high, consisting of eleven stories, with a floor area of about 10.5 square meters.
The stupa has a hexagonal shape. Each level features six arched doors, and within each doorway stands a statue of Amitabha Buddha carved from white precious stone. In total, there are sixty-six statues of varying sizes. This number, together with the name Six Perfection symbolizes the six noble practices (paramitas) of the Bodhisattva.
